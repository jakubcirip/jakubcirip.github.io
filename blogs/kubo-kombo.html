Motivation and Idea

First of all, I would like to offer you an insight into what motivated me for this project.

Why would someone want a guitar combo?
The answer is simple, they want to play their guitar with a decent sound.
But why not just buy one, but make one? - I like to make simple things more complicated;)
The basic idea was to use 2 45W speakers that I had left over from the original setup.

In the next section we will see the assembly of components for the project.

List of compoments

  • 2x 45W 8Ω reproductors
  • MeanWell LRS-150-24, Power supply 24V, 6.5A, 150W
  • 2x Preamplifier Tone Board, Hi-Fi Audio Amplifier Module DC 12–24V
  • Audio stereo amplifier HX-M189 - 2 x 50W
  • 2x JFET buffer
  • Combo case made of plastic, wood and damping material

Combo Design - proposal and solution

The dimensions must be designed so that all the modules of the combo are placed inside it and are easily accessible for assembly and serviceability.

The dimensions of the box are:
420 x 320 x 230
The 420 width is sufficient to comfortably place two 185 diameter speakers side by side.
In the middle of the box and on the sides I designed various points for attaching all the components and modules.

Combo case manufacturing

Proposal of the inner wall

For better sound properties, my friend, an expert in the field of audio equipment, recommended that I insert an inner wall that will separate the speakers from the electrical equipment and at the same time place damping material on it (on the side near the speakers) so that the sound spreads better outwards from the combo.
This wall will also be useful for placing electrical component (preamps, buffers, aplifier and DC conventor).

In the next picture you can see how it looks inside with this wall (without all components).


JFET Buffer


Impedance Buffer

A buffer is a circuit that isolates one part of a system from another, often acting as a bridge between a high impedance source and a low impedance load.
It ensures signal integrity by preventing loading, distortion, or signal loss, while usually providing current gain.
For the purpose of our combo, it separates the guitar from the preamp module, providing a high input impedance at the input of the preamp module.


Main Characteristic of JFET buffer:

  • It preserved the signal from the guitar without losing quality. It shapes the tone without change.
  • Matches the impedance of the guitar and the preamp input.
  • Amplifies current, not voltage.
  • Separates the guitar from the preamp – the preamp circuit does not affect the guitar pickup.
  • Preserves the dynamics and natural tone of the guitar.

Buffer Scheme

In this phase, the first step was design of buffer and scheme drawing. The testing program also helped me with simulation odf buffer and measurement the values of voltige. For JFET 'follower' beffer is really important to set up the right operating point of JFET transistor.
The DC operating point is the voltage and current in transistor without input signal.
It defines transistor behaviour during signal processing.
If the operating point is set up incorrectly it can cause,
that transistor will be 'close' -> means doesn't work,
or will be overloaded -> which leads to signal distortion, noise or overheating
On the first picture we can see the simple buffer with voltage divider for transistor Gate (this voltage help us to set the right operating point). The buffer is without input and output signals and capacitors.

Buffer schematic — without input/output capacitors


On the second picture I added an input source (AC) (which represents the source from the guitar) trought a 1uF input capacitor and an output signal from a transistor Source going trought 4,7uF output capacitor. Using simulation mode we can see the voltage readings on the voltmeters.
